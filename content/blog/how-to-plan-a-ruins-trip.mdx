A ruins trip rewards planning more than almost any other, because the best
sites are time-sensitive (light, heat, crowds) and ticket-controlled. Plan
backwards from the ruins, not the hotels.

## Step 1: Pick a theme

The strongest ruins trips follow a thread: Roman Britain, the dissolved
abbeys, Magna Graecia in Sicily, the Maya lowlands. A theme gives the route
logic and stops it becoming a tiring checklist of unrelated stones.

## Step 2: Map the sites

Open the [map](/), filter to your region, and see how the ruins cluster.
Geography decides the trip: a Yorkshire abbey circuit is a day; ruins across a
country is a week. Plan realistic travel and never schedule two major sites for
the same afternoon — each deserves its own.

## Step 3: Get the timing right

Aim for shoulder season — spring or autumn — and within each day target the
first and last opening hours. Cool air, thin crowds, and low raking light that
reveals carving invisible at noon. This single habit transforms ruin
photography and comfort.

## Step 4: Book the timed-ticket sites

The headline ruins (the Colosseum, Stonehenge, Pompeii in peak season) sell
timed entry that sells out. Reserve these first and build each day around the
fixed slot; smaller sites are walk-up and make good flexible filler.

## Step 5: Add interpretation

Budget time for the site museum (where the finds and reconstructions live) and
book a licensed guide for the one or two anchor sites. A good guide turns a
confusing field into a city; the museum supplies the dates and context.

## Step 6: Sequence for stamina

Front-load the largest, hottest, most walking-intensive sites while you are
fresh; put museum or town days between them. "Ruin fatigue" is real — three
huge sites in a row blur into one.

## Step 7: Pack for fieldwork

Sturdy closed shoes, sun hat and high-SPF cream, more water than feels
sensible, a light layer for shade and ruined-chapel chill, and a paper backup
map. Shade is usually nonexistent — plan around it.

## Step 8: Know the rules

Stay on paths and off walls, never touch frescoes or mosaics, drones are
banned at most heritage sites, and removing any fragment is illegal almost
everywhere. Respect farmland access where ruins sit on working land.

## Put it together

Theme, then map, then timing, then booked tickets, then stamina. Open the
[map](/), draw the cluster, and let the ancient geography write a better
itinerary than any guidebook loop.
